Week 1

Team 6 learns about unity and excitedly unbox our VR and AR headsets. We are pumped.

Posted by VRcap Team 6 on April 10, 2020

Learning Unity

The project has started off right where it should, with a health amount of Unity tutotrials. Our team members are fairly new to unity and to get things rolling we started with a tutorial called RollaBall, giving us exposure to fundamental unity api functions for working with game objects within the editor.

After having fun creating our first game in unity we learned how to set up a scene in the editor for various VR/AR platforms via Unity's asset store.

Taking the Leap

The entire team was beside ourselves when it was announced that every memeber of the capstone would recieve a Magic Leap to partake in an experiment to test the capabilities of remote AR collaboaration during the pandemic quarintine. It is amazing to have the oppotunity to put this technology to the test and workout ways to incorporate it into our projects.

Embarking on our Quest

Exploring the Oculus Quest is another first experience for the team. The mobility and accessability of the Quest is of great interest to the group and we are excited to how we can use this invative new frontier of wireless VR to give

.

Wind in the sails

Brainstorming ideas for our project's focus was more difficult than we anticipated having both VR and AR headsets at our disposal. During the initial discussions, it was clear that we wanted to create an application that would allow users to be social with one anther and use the XR technologies to bridge the isolation that now effects everyone.

During our discussions we discovered a common liking for the outdoors and we are fortunate to live in an area with many beautiful natural features. From the inspiration of the Puget Sound, Hands on Deck was born.

Team 6 is very excited to be taking on this project and what we can accomplish with it.
